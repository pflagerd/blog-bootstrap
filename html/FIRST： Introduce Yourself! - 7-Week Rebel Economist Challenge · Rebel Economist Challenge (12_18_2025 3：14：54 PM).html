Welcome and please introduce yourself
Hi, and welcome to the new "short lectures" course: 15-30 minute lectures every Wednesday at 1pm New York time, followed by 30-45 minutes of discussion. Here's the link for the lectures. Click on it 5-10 minutes before 1pm on Wednesdays, and I'll start the lecture at 5 minutes past the hour: https://us06web.zoom.us/j/3366324240?pwd=YjdLaENqem9oRjZTRzgrV0dwQVBnQT09 I know why I'm here: the mundane is to give the lectures, the deep is that I really want to drag economics into the real world from the fantasy world that it currently inhabits. I've been trying to do that ever since I was an 18-year-old 1st year student at Sydney University in 1971, and these lectures are a means to share what I've learnt in the intervening half a century (gawd it makes me feel even older to put it that way!). Now I'd like to know why you're here. Make a comment on this post and share your own story (you'll get mine in slightly more detail in the first lecture tomorrow). Let us know where you're from, whether you're studying right now, etc.; it's possible that you'll find someone else from your own State/City/University/Workplace here, so if you're comfortable with sharing such details, please do so. Lastly, if you want to get up to speed upon your arrival here, please read the attached book manuscript Rebuilding Economics from the Top Down.
